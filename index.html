About

Team Cybrotics, of Government College of Engineering, Aurangabad, is a group of robotics enthusiasts who have been fabricating robots since 2010. We have been participating in ABU- Robocon, an International Robotic Contest for last 9 years. The National level competition (Robocon India) of ABU Robocon is being organised by Doordarshan and the winner of this national level competition gets the chance to represent India at International level. Every year, we fabricate a robot to meet the demands of the given problem statement and successfully complete all the tasks assigned to it. Also we, as a part of RAISe Society (Robotics Association and Innovation Society), organise workshop on Robotics and automation in our college to nurture and encourage the young enthusiasts from all branches in this field.

    2018 Robot
    Previous Next
    • Theme - NEM CON (throwing shuttlecock)
    • Venue - MIT Pune(Balewadi Stadium)

    For ABU Robocon 2018, the theme released by international host Vietnam was 'Nem Con: The Festival Wishing Happiness and Prosperity'. According to this problem statement, each team was supposed to fabricate 2 robots that is 1 manual and other automatic. The manual bot was given the task to pick up the shuttlecocks or the rack carrying shuttlecock and hand it over to the automatic bot which was given task to throw that shuttlecock holding it on the keeping point 250mm away from the spherical shape on the tail through two 800mm diameter rings from three different throwing zones.
    Innovations that were brought about in robot model this year:
    • Use of belt drive for motion transmission in high torque high rpm chassis motors
    • Use of disc brake to stop the arm at required position and economical pneumatic gripper.
    • Use of Microsoft Kinect V2.0 for depth image processing.
    • Use of MATLAB for design and simulation of both robots.
    The team from GECA, entering its 8th year of formation was given tagline-‘Keep it Simple’. With a new tagline and the old expertise and enthusiasm, the team this year consisted of: Utkarsh Dange (Team Captain), Prayush Kale (Team Vice Captain), Tanmay Chaudhari, Utkarsh Kolte, Vishakha Kate, Abhishek Sakharkar, Pratiksha Hivarekar, Swapnil Pimple, Pritamkumar Anap, Saurabh Satpute, Archit Wadalkar, Rushabh Solanki, Nilesh Abhang, Vaibhav Jadhav, Priti Songade, Sangharsh Bhawar, Angha Deo, Aniket Murtadak, Mrunali Deshpande, Anshul Pandey, Malhar Garje, Aishwarya Balkhande and Snehashish Saha.
    The National Contest was held from 1st March to 3rd March, 2018 at Balewadi Stadium, Pune. The team won both its initial matches in league stage with IIT Patna and VSSUT Burla and qualified for super league standing 7th in league stage. In super league, the team achieved ‘Rong bay’ in both the games and secured position in top 15. Also the team won prestigious MATHWORKS 1st runner up award with Rs 25k because admirable usage of MATLAB in stimulation.

    2017 Robot
    Previous Next
    • Theme - ASOBI: The Landing Disc
    • Venue - Shri Shiv Chhatrapati Krida Sankul

    For ABU Robocon 2017, the theme released by international host Japan was 'Asobi: The Landing Disc'. In this problem statement, the robot is to place polyurethane foam discs on given 7 spots. It is based on the traditional Japanese game of Tosenkyo. As the foam discs used in the theme were irregular and non-uniform, this theme emphasised solving real life problems using robotic solutions.
    Innovations that were brought about in robot model this year:
    • Use of rotary encoders for speed control using feedback,
    • Fabrication and interfacing of encoder setup locally using slotted disc and IR sensors,
    • Use of belt drive for motion transmission at high rpm,
    • Machining of most components done locally in college workshop by students,

    The team from GECA, entering its 7th year of formation, was rechristened as 'TEAM CYBROTICS' this year. With a new name and the old expertise and enthusiasm, the team this year consisted of: Ashher Mirza (Team Captain), Abhishek Sakharkar (Manual Bot Operator), Nikhil Ekbote, Dhananjay Kulkarni, Sanjay Hiwrale, Akshay Kale, Aishwarya Wagh, Vishakha Kate, Utkarsh Dange, Utkars h Kolte, Prayush Kale, Tanmay Chaudhary, Saurabh Satpute, Archit Wadalkar, Pritam Anap, Divyansh Gautam, Swapnil Pimple, Pratiksha Hivrale, Aditya Mahamuni, Angha Deo, Sangharsh Bhawar & Vaijesh Kotwal.     2016 Robot
    Previous Next
    • Theme - Chai-Yo : Clean Energy Recharging the World
    • Venue - Shri Shiv Chhatrapati Krida Sankul, Pune

    For ABU Robocon 2016, the theme released was 'Clean Energy: Recharging the World'. Two bots, one without an actuator to drive, were made.
    Various newer elements like Electric Ducted Fans, Pneumatic circuits, Timing Belts and Pulleys were used in making the bots. Extensive research was also carried out in the field of Digital Image Processing. For propulsion of the bot without the driving actuator, permanent Neodymium magnets were used, which proved to be the unique stand out feature of the bot.
    This year, team GECA was made up of these students: Bharat Chauhan(Team Captain), Nikhil Ekbote (Manual Bot Operator), Tanveer Aga, Abhijeet Berad, Sakshi Jain, Dhananjay Kulkarni, Atul Farakte, Sanjay Hiwrale, Akshay Kale, Ashher Mirza, Aishwarya Wagh, Shailendra Kirtikar, Gaurav Rayamane, Vishakha Kate, Utkarsh Dange, Utkarsh Kolte, Prayush Kale, Tanmay Chaudhary, Abhishek Sakharkar..
    Principal Dr. P.S. Adwani, Faculty Advisors Dr. Vandana Deodhar-Kulkarni, Dr. R.K.Shrivastav, Prof. S.S. Agrawal, Dr. Archana Thosar, Dr.Nilima Kolhare provided valuable guidance to the students' efforts in the endeavor.
    The national event of ABU Robocon was held from 3rd to 5th March, 2016 in Balewadi, Pune where about 105 teams from various institutes participate. Team GECA, banking on its accuracy, cleared the League Stage with flying colours. In the Super-League stage, even though matched against a tough opponent, the team won some close contests to qualify for the Quarterfinals. However, some technical glitches in the quarterfinal led to loss of crucial time and the team faced a loss in the knockout contest.
    Overall, team GECA was ranked 8th all over India. Additionally, in recognition of the unique design of the bot, team GECA won the ‘Best Innovative Design Award’.
